## whet

*whet* is a verb. Basically means to make sharper a sword or knife. But you can whet abstract things like curiosity or apettite.

- I whetted a cheap knife with super expensive whetstones.
- The smell of food whets my appetite.
- We must whet our courage to be prepared for battle.

## curb
*curb* is countable noun. It is a rule or something that limits something else. You can say is *limitar* or *impedir* in Spanish. A restraint is synonym.

- Put a curb to your lack of empathy.
- Put a curb to that anger of yours.

*curb* can be use as verb too.
- I'm fairly sure this new measures will curb bot messages.
- We must curb ourselves on not doing something rash.
- Curb that victim talk.
- This measures will curb global warming.

## rung
*rung* is a noun. It is one of the horizontal supports on a ladder for a person's foot. The thing is... I think this could be use for ladder. *escalón* in Spanish.

- It was just the first rung on the ladder.
- She rose a few rungs on the social ladder.
- That ladder has like 30 rungs.

## pant
*pant* is a intransitive verb. It means to breath loudly, like when you are running and feeling tired.

- She panted a lot while we were running.
- That panic attack cause him to pant unceasingly.
- We go panting for things we don't need.
- "Draw?" He panted.

Come to think of it... it could be use as  as synonym for chase.

- He went panting for his dog.

## bicker
*bicker* is an intransitive verb. To argue about things that are not important.

- They bicker about the most pointless things.
- They are bickering over whether we have free will or not.
- That bickering of yours is a waste of time.
- Curb your bickering.

## heap
*heap* is a transitive verb. It means to make a messy pile of things.

- Heap up commendation and become a slave.
- Several plastic bottles were heaped on the floor.
- He heaped up deep friendships during his life.
- Over time she heaped up enemies.

## treacherous
*treacherous* is an adjective. Something dangerous, but it doesn't look like it is.

- His treacherous made me suspicious.
- Smoking is treacherous act.
- Alcohol is a treacherous beverage.
- What you did looks right, but it is actually treacherous.
- A treacherous luck.
