## flock

A **flock** could be a group of birds, goats or sheep.

- The shepherd is bringing his flock down from the high mountains.
- That flock of birds is numerous.
- Groups of goats could be called flocks or herds.

## estrange

**estrange** means to become no longer close to someone who you were formerly friendly. Translating the word to Spanish gives "alejarse". It is a verb and it is transitive.

- My job estranges me from my own family.
- He estranges himself from his friends due to the time he spends playing videogames.

I have seen that you estrange yourself from *someone*, or *somebody* estranges you from *someone* else.

However, you can be *estranged* from *something*.

- Drinking alcohol estranged him from the church.
- It makes me feel happy to be estranged from my previous life.
- That decision estranged her from her loved ones.

... or *something* can be *estranged* from something.

- Those ideas are estranged from reality.
- That life I had of squander is completely estranged from the present.
- Anger estranges good judgement.

## conformity

It is behaviour that is standard or acceptable. A noun.

- Too much conformity feeds the fear of dissaproval.

... or it could be in accordance to something.

- In conformity to Youtube Terms of Service...
- Bees fly, in conformity to laws of aviation.
- Our products are in conformity to safety standards.
