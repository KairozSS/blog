What the title says.

I suck at writing. Whenever I go back to look at things I previously wrote, I feel a bit embarrased. The idea I had in my head doesn't look great once I put it in words. Sometimes I simply can't convey whatever is in my mind and if I do, the text doesn't flow, it is uninteresting or repetitive.

I think to be able to express yourself in an interesting manner is key if you want to make interesting content, especially if those require long narration. At least, I can see that those video-essays that are good have behind good writing. You can make good research, find things that nobody else has not been able to find or make an unbelievable claim like "the moon is made of cheese", but if you don't know how to present the information, nobody is going to pay attention.

You can talk about things that at first glance look uninteresting like krebs cycle and still make them appealing if you know how to present the information. This brings the question whether ideas are inherently interesting or you make them interesting. My view is that you can make any idea interesting if you know how to present it, but some of them are more difficult to make them appealing.

Writing is a skill, so if I want to become good at writing, I have to write a lot. That ability of putting your thoughts into words is something that has to be trained. Think of it as you imagining flashy karate moves that you watched in a movie. Being able to imagine those moves even with total detail doesn't mean that you are able to perform them. The same thing can be said about writing, it is a skill that has to be trained.

So I'll keep writing until I suck less at it, it is a bit embarrasing what I sometimes produce, I mean, I look stupid, but I guess it is a small price to pay.
