## pernicious
- The way he talked about it was pernicious.
- That video had pernicious effects.
- It was a pernicious spead of disinformation.

## felicity
- What I do is my highest felicity.
- They live in a bubble of felicity.
- Having bought a house for my family causes me felicity.

## phony
An *adjective*. It means fake or false. Something intended to trick people.

- A phony relationship.
- She uses a completely phony personality in her videos.
- That phony scam made him lose a lot of money.
- A phony driver license.

## enervated
An *adjective*. Feeling tired and weak.

- That physical demanding work made me felt enervated at the end of the day.
- Math class is enervating.
- Editing videos is an enevarting task.

## disparage
A *transitive verb*.

- These losers always disparage whoever is better than them. Pretty much everyone.
- It is a bad idea to disparage people behind their backs.

## altogether
*adverb*. Man, this one has several ways of using it. I'll stick with just one for now. It is used when something has stopped or finished completely.

- Everyone of us finished our homework altogether, so we went to a bar.
- The earthquake stopped altogether.
- The sun began to rise and the stars have already dissapeared altogether.

## endowment
*countable noun*. If someone has an endowment of a particular quality or ability, they possess it naturally.

- An endowment of reason.

It is also a gift of money you give to an institution.

- The foundation received an endowment of 1.5 million dollars.
- The endowment they received was tiny, nevertheless it was something.

## covertly
*adverb*. In a secret or hidden way.

- I read the novel covertly during class.
- I made my way into the building convertly.
- My colleagues disparage the boss covertly.

## uptight
An *adjective* In Spanish it means *tenso*. It looks like it is a common word. However this word also applies for people who get annoyed easily.

- I don't know why, but her attitude was uptight, anything I said, she yell at me.
- I was so uptight, because that exam was coming.
- Before a presentation, she gets uptight.
- The atmosphere of that place made him feel uptight.

## kindred
*adjective*. Very similar to one person or thing.

- Those two are kindred spirits.
- Kindred artists.
- They both made videos of kindred topics.
