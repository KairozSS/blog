*You become stronger trying to do things you believe you can't do.*

This is where everything starts.

With a man, who dreams and dreams of an unattainable goal and has no way of winning.

I say unattainable, the truth is, that's just a part of myself who thinks that.

I have a plan of making a Youtube Channel.

Most people find that silly. The idea of growing a channel, building a brand and become relevant. After all... you get this feeling that, just a few people succeed on that path. It is time consuming too. Why invest so much time in something that probably won't turn out well? To put it bluntly, that goal is akin to form your own rock band and hope someday you become successful, traveling around the world for your concert tour.

*An impossible dream.*

Although a part of myself doubts, another thinks that it's an interesting and fun problem because it is challenging. It makes my mind spin its wheels trying its best to figure out how to make things work. It's a problem that overwhelms me, I can't even begin to think how to approach it and, at the same time, it fires me up. The stakes are high and I know it is something that is going to push me over the limit, which is something I find thrilling. So how do I do it? How do I succeed? Especially me, a man who is in a position which makes things harder or even unwinnable. Several reasons pop up in my mind that make me not to want to do it, and despite that, *I only need one reason to do it*. And the solution to fighting against that hesitation is simple: *You simply try something.* *Anything you can think of*, and you see how it goes. Regardless of whether it works or not, you will get some new knowledge or... in the way I phrased it at the beginning: "You become stronger".

There's always going to be a first time for doing a particular thing. Before that, you are going to feel that you don't have enough preparation and that you are not ready. And the truth is you most likely never going to feel ready the first time. Maybe not even the second or third time.

So you take a step and become a bit stronger. You either fail or win, but regardless of the outcome, you'll become stronger. Take the knowledge you gain and use it for a better attempt next time. In a matter of time, you'll be in a place that you would have never thought possible at the beginning. 

*So why wouldn't you want to become stronger?* Because you are afraid? Because of the pain? The cost? The time? All those excuses are valid and they become meaningless once you have a reason to want to do things. Despite of all those reasons for not doing it, you go with all those demons in your head, do it, and become stronger.

It looks impossible, the demons whisper to you to stop. You look like a fool, trying to take on a challenge he can't handle. And it's precisely *the will to continue*, one of the things that will make you stronger. The mental fortitude you get ignoring those demons. After all, heros are just fools who got away with what they wanted.

So remember, whenever there is something you want to do, something difficult because you believe you lack the skills, the talent, the preparation or resources remember:

*You become stronger trying to do things you believe you can't do.*
