My first video on Youtube is going to be about Youtube itself.

It's going to be about how it became big in the first place. So that means I have to take a look at its foundation and find out what made its numbers explode. Also, I'm going to research its history from 2005 to 2006. 

The reason why I'm picking the topic is to familiarize myself with the place.

People usually try to find information about "how to crack the Youtube Algorithm" and they take an approach where they try to "humor it", but I don't believe that's the right thing to do. If you take a quick look to the history of Youtube, you realize that The Recommendation Algorithm is a tool that Youtube developed to make easier for people to find content they want to watch. It solves the early problem they had when their library of videos began to grow exponentially: With so many videos, people weren't sure what to watch or they couldn't find the content they like.

There was a time when The Algorithm didn't exist and videos still blow up using the most ancient method: Word of mouth. The reason people heard on the grapevine about a particular video was because the video was good. No Algorithm, no SEO, no bots and you have to consider that it was harder to find a particular video with the search function they had at the time.

I think exploring the history of Youtube is going to give me an insight on why the place behaves the way it behaves and what it is looking for, what it expects from me. In some way, I'm trying to understand its "heart". What is Youtube about? If I get a bit of knowledge about that matter, I think the *"what to do"* to grow a channel and *"how to do it"* will become more evident.

The video I'm going to make is a way to practice how to make an interesting video. How to present information in a way that keeps attention and it is memorable. In other words make a good video. Unfortunately I lack experience in that matter, so it is highly likely I won't be able to nail it the first time. It's part of the process though. I'll try my best to make it good, but if the result is not good enough I'll have to accept it, figure out what went wrong and improve upon that next time. That's the way you become good at something.

A lot of people say that in order to make it on Youtube, you have to make good videos. But nobody tells you how to make good videos, instead, you get advice about The Algorithm. I think that it is expected that you have to figure out that by yourself and find your own formula, but I'm pretty sure there should be a list of things that are proven not to work. Things that make a video boring. Given that Youtube is relatively new, probably you can't find information about how to make videos that are engaging. So the route is to look for something similar, for instance figure out what are the guidelines to make good movies, good ads or how to write better. Anything related to storytelling sounds useful to me too.

The video is going to be about Youtube, but the channel, I don't think it will be about Youtube. One common problem I see is that once you establish that your channel is about an specific topic, it becomes difficult to branch out to other things without losing subscribers and views. After a few years talking about the same things, I'm pretty sure that you eventually grow tired of them and you have this need to talk about other things.

So the current plan right now is to establish early that my channel is about a lot of things and at the same time provide something that is unique or rare to find in other places. It has to be something that is desired too. This could be the style in my videos, something you don't find in other videos and people like. Or it could be the fact that I am the one making the videos and people hear MY thoughts, MY opinions. That's the path where people get attached to your personality and watch anything you upload because it's you. I still don't have idea what I'm going to do and I still need to flesh it out a bit. 

A problem I see is that the point of establishing what your channel is about is to make easier for people to find the content they are looking for. If you are a cooking channel it is expected you upload recipes and people subscribe to the channel because that's what they are looking for, recipes. But if you are an "anything channel" you are unpredictable. Why subscribe to a channel if you don't know what to expect from it? In fact if you think a bit about it, the topic of the channel could be the *unique* thing that no one else is offering and the reason people are looking for your videos. Again, I still need to think a bit about the niche/topic/theme of my channel.

Anyway, I think I'll stop here for now. I'll write more about what I'm going to do in the next entry when I find the time.
