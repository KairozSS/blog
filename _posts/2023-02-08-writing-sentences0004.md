## whet
- I whetted my courage and went into the unknown.
- Whet that bravery of yours and fight!

## curb
- His stubborness curbed his chances to meet other people.
- Her lack of talent curbs her ability to succeed.
- Faith curbs people's fear to death.
- Put a curb to your drinking habits!

## bicker
- All that is just pointless bickering.
- Those people bicker about politics at the table.
- They bickered about a pointless drama on Twitter.

## flock
- You can use the word flock for a group of sheeps, goats and birds.

## commendation
- Most people are thirsty for commendation.
- Nowadays commendation from people on social media is kind of a asset.

## rung
- Just another rung on the ladder.

## exhort
- You have to exhort all those investors to invest in the company.
- She exhort us to do our very best.

## hefty
- A hefty number of flowers.
- A hefty cat.
- A hefty blow to the stomach.
- A hefty number of people.

## likewise
- -"Take care" - "Likewise"
- In order for students to get their Bachelor degree, they need to write a thesis. Likewise Master students have to write a longer thesis.
- Touch typing is a mechanical skill. Likewise pronouncing a foreign language correctly requires a lot of repetion.

## caprice
- Her caprice is utterly annoying.
- The caprices of fate.
- His decision was completely motivated by caprice.

## hog
- A loud hog.
- The hefty hog was sleeping.

## deliverance
- We are praying for God's deliverance.
- So we ought to do the best for ourselves to get deliverance from out inner demons.

## taxanomy
- A taxonomy of frogs.
- The Internet developed a taxonomy of memes.

## pernicious
*pernicious* is an adjective.
- What he did was a pernicious act.
- A pernicious man who deserved to be exhiled.

## felicity
It is a *noun* that means, great happinness.
- My carreer and my family are my highest felicity.
- Her children are her felicity.
- To be set free from my pernicious mind is the felicity I am aiming for.
- ...and we humans desire everlasting felicity.

## pampered
*pampered* is an adjective.

- With the coming of techonology today's children are pampered.
- Rich kids are so pampered to do anything for themselves.
- After a shower and a night good sleep I felt completely pampered.
