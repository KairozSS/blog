## bewail
It is a *verb*. It means to express regret, sorrow, dissapointment over something. The verb is *transitive*.

- In the middle of the dark, he bewailed his life.
- She bewailed her decision.

## presumptuousness
It is a *noun*. It is an attitude where you show little respect for others. Also you have lots of pride for yourself.

- That presumptuousness of hers is annoying.
- His actions show a lot of presumptuousness.
- presumptuousness is a delusion, at the end you can't better than everyone else.

## conniving
An *adjective*. Used for a person that secretly tries to hurt others. I would say it is a synonym for treacherous.

- A conniving politician.

The *verb* connive means to secretly plan something immoral or illegal. It is like *conspire*. But there is another one that means ignoring a behavior and because of that it looks you approve that behavior.

- These rebels were conniving against the state.

## prolific
An *adjective*. Someone who is prolific produces a lot of something.

- A prolific bird species.
- A prolific composer.
- A prolific content creator.

## disparage
A *verb*. When you disparage someone, it means that you speak about them in a way that shows you don't have a good opinion about them.

- Society disparages those people who don't work.
- Tom disparages Bob in secret.
- Twitter disparages anyone who don't agree with their political views.
- That clown disparages the group's translation without any reason.
