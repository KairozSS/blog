It's English. 

After all Youtube is an American website.
After all lots of people understand English.
After all lots of people speak English.
After all whether or not is your native language you must understand it to get up to date information about a certain topic.
After all whether or not is your native language you must speak it if you want to reach a larger audience.
After all English is the lingua franca.

English is not my native language. In fact, I've decided to write in this language because I want to improve my ability to output it, because eventually, that skill is going to be required.

Again, English is Youtube's default language.

If you want to reach a good amount of people, your videos must be in English, but if it isn't your native language and you actually struggle to speak it (like me) you have to get creative.

My Youtube content is going to be first in Spanish (my native language), the reason is that I don't want to be limited to what kind of content I can do due to language. For example, I can't make unscripted content in English without the quality suffering. The content would be filled of a ton of pauses, "ehh...", very basic commentary because I'm limited on my ability to express complex ideas and even basic sentences would be a fight on its own trying to get the pronunciation right. 

Think about it, if it is already difficult to provide interesting commentary in your native language, imagine doing it in a foreign one like Portuguese.

Because I can't make unscripted content in English, that means I can't live stream, make some low-effort video reaction, make some kind of podcast or content with long unscripted commentary or even interact with an English speaking audience.

So I must not ignore my native language, it provides me at least a possibility of making something interesting. My family and friends speak my language too and they will probably be the first real audience I ever have.

So what about English being Youtube's default language? Well this is what I've been thinking.

I'll dub my videos.

That's right. I can't output the language well in real time, but I'm pretty sure I can do it well with enough time and takes. Naturally it is double the amount of work, but I think it is worth it considering that your potential audience gets larger and dubbing the videos helps you to learn a language that you must know.

I considered subtitles and call it a day, but I know people hate to read a video, especially if the content is fast-paced and the subtitles change every half-second.

There are several problems with dubbing videos though. For example, I know that if you don't do it right, the content could end up "feeling" different. The lack of background noise and the crystal clear audio recording creates an artifical atmosphere. That without mentioning lip-sync.

Another limitation is that if you have two versions of the same video, how are you going to upload them? Both in the same channel? I don't think that would look good.

The solution for that one is to create two channels: One for Spanish and one for English. I don't like this solution completely, but that's the only thing I can do for now. I'm aware that there is a beta functionality that allows you to put several audio tracks in one video, but that's only available to big content creators.

If I had to guess, that feature is going to be meta when it becomes available to everyone. Instead of your audience being filtered by the language you use in your content, now it would be in your capacity to make the entire world your potential audience. That coupled with the current developments of speech-to-text and text-to-speech AI will make translating content much easier.

Going back to the present, dubbing yourself the content is -as I mentioned before- a ton of work. Do you release both versions of the same video at the same time? Or do you release the original first then work on the dub and release the dub later? I don't have a confident answer for this one.

There's a ton of tiny things like that that come up to my mind, questions and matters I'm not completely sure how I would deal with.

I'm pretty sure you, *YOU*, can think of more problems if you were to follow an approach like this one.

But instead of thinking all the time about these potential problems, I'll just do it and figure out things along the way. *After all* just thinking about it, analyzing every tiny possibility and trying to craft the perfect plan won't get me anywhere.

Wish me luck.
